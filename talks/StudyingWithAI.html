Results are impressive

AlphaGeometry, 17 Jan 2024: An Olympiad-level AI system for [school tasks] geometry


  • Solves 25 out of 30 latest IMO geometry problems
  • The previous best method only solves 10 problems
  • By using existing symbolic engines on a diverse set of random theorem premises, they extracted 100 million synthetic theorems and their proofs, many with more than 200 proof steps, four times longer than the average proof length of olympiad theorems
  • Then produced nearly 10 million synthetic proof steps that construct auxiliary points, reaching beyond the scope of pure symbolic deduction
I am thrilled to present to you the results of a groundbreaking study published on the 17th of January, 2024, in 'Nature.' This study introduces us to AlphaGeometry, an Olympiad-level artificial intelligence system designed specifically to tackle complex school geometry tasks. Now, let's quantify 'impressive,' shall we? AlphaGeometry has achieved a remarkable feat by successfully solving 25 out of the 30 International Mathematical Olympiad, or IMO, geometry problems. To put this achievement into perspective, the previous best method could only solve 10 problems and average human doesn't solve even one problem. But how did AlphaGeometry reach such proficiency? By harnessing existing symbolic engines and applying them to a diverse set of random theorem premises, the AI managed to extract an astonishing 100 million synthetic theorems and their corresponding proofs. Many of these proofs contain over 200 proof steps, which is four times longer than the average proof length found in Olympiad theorems. This is no small feat and speaks volumes about the AI's deep understanding of geometry. Moreover, AlphaGeometry produced nearly 10 million synthetic proof steps that involve constructing auxiliary points. This is particularly noteworthy because it signifies a leap beyond the realm of pure symbolic deduction. The AI is not just following a set of rules; it's exhibiting an understanding of geometry that allows it to reach solutions in a way that's reminiscent of human intuition and creativity. The implications of this advancement are profound. They not only revolutionize the way we approach mathematical problems but also pave the way for future applications of AI in education and beyond.

Pros and cons of ChatGPT in studying IT

Useful cases

  • helps answer questions for tools with poor or unreadable documentation (for example, it can tell you how to start using GDB)
  • gives good and quick examples for some JSONs, XMLs with the necessary characteristics, or some fixtures to fill in the database
  • helps to overcome the fear of a ‚Äúblank slate‚Äù


What it doesn't handle well

  • any task where you need to understand deeply the general context of the whole project
  • not typical problems in not so popular languages: if you ask to solve a dynamic programming problem in Haskell, it gives funny answers
  • for different mathematical facts a lot of the GPT-generated proofs are incorrect
So what are the pros and cons of using ChatGPT in studying?
Let's begin with the USEFUL CASES.
It HELPS answer questions for tools with poor or inaccessible documentation (for example, it can tell you how to start using GNU Debugger).
It PROVIDES nice and quick examples for some JSONs and other objects, or some values to fill in the database.
ONE of the main points: it helps to overcome the fear of a ‚Äúblank slate.‚Äù
And WHAT it doesn't handle well:
Definitely it is ANY TASK where you have a large general context of the whole project.
ALSO, it is non-typical problems in not so popular areas: if you ask to solve a dynamic programming problem in Haskell, it gives funny answers, which are totally wrong.
CRUCIAL for us: for different mathematical facts a lot of the GPT-generated proofs are incorrect.

Let me switch gears second time and share my own workflow for teaching and lecture preparation.
FIRSTLY, I usually work in PyCharm complemented with the TeXiFy IDEA plugin. This combination brings together LaTeX, Python, and JavaScript all in one place. It allows using powerful autocompletion and inspections of PyCharm, and inverse search from pdf.
NEXT, let's discuss the AI Assistant. It is capable of assisting with translations in a broader sense. AI helper is not just restricted to translating languages; it can convert formats, elaborate short ideas into detailed descriptions, and even to oral speech, facilitate auto tests creation, and more.
THIRDLY, I'm using the GitHub Copilot plugin. It offers practical pieces of code, draft solutions, and generally helps speed up the writing of text content for my instructions or lectures.
LAST but not least, the Grazie Pro plugin checks for typos and polishes long sentences. Its purpose is to ensure your text is clean, concise, and clear before reaching your audience.
Here I'd like to show the concrete example from my own experience. It is producing the problem for the International Mathematical Competition.
First of all, I have chosen an appropriate problem from ancient times of my teaching at Novosibirsk State University. There I organized math contests with tricky olimpiad tasks. So I need to translate the problem from Russian to English and to make a Latex format from Microsoft Word.
You see, with the help of AI assistant; I can do it in a few seconds. Here I simply copy-paste the answer to the working area. Then I check the result and look at the Grazie suggestions. Here I don't accept it, because it suggests something strange about additional dot in the text after the word "elements", but often it helps to polish the text.
Then I compile Tex sources and look at PDF file.
Then I need to write a solution, so I think through the solution and type it in Latex format. So firstly, I write x^2 = 0, then latex Rightarrow command goes. Here you can see that GitHub Copilot helps me to write the solution very quickly; it even makes simple calculation with math formulas.
Then I write the second part of the solution. Here I need to prove that the product being equal to zero is commutative. So that ab=0 if and only if ba=0. You see, sometimes I disagree with the Copilot's suggestions, but often it helps. Eventually, I check everything, recompile Tex sources and get the final PDF file done. Really fast, effective and convenient workflow, at least for me. Maybe you will consider using something similar for your own practice.
